Bonsai Exhibition

The activity was a bonsai exhibition that was held at Silliman University Arineo Art Gallery. Its first official opening was on November 6, 2024, and it ended on November 23, 2024. The event was organized by the Negros Island BOnsai and Suiseki Society (NIBOSS). The main objective of this exhibition is to showcase the different bonsai art that was created by local artists. During the activity, I was a mere spectator observing the different artworks in the gallery since it was a requirement for our GE6 subject. I took my time roaming and looking for potential artwork to add to my assignment. I chose an artwork that I think is distinctive and has a deep meaning. I also considered the requirement for the GE10 that the student should spend an hour in the event for it to be considered an official event in my journal. I went to the event on November 15 from 2:30 and left around 3:30 but I did not see the organizers only students who were also required to join and the person responsible for our attendance. This event is essential for the bonsai art to be exposed more in the region and to help the artist gain the recognition that they deserve.

During the event I did not gain any skills, but as I explored the different artworks, I began to appreciate the patience of the artist in creating those bonsai. My first expectation of the event was to find a bonsai that I believe has a deep meaning so that I can add it to my assignment in GE6. Fortunately, I found a piece that I think was an artwork worthy to be added to my assignment. I won't elaborate more on it since it's a 300-word essay but it was more about the resilience of mother nature and how the artwork can be reflected in the perspective of humans. The only thing that surprised me was the ambiance of the gallery. Together with the bonsai artworks it gives a feeling of being in ancient China.

When I was roaming, I began to ponder on the idea of how much time and effort this artist put into creating these artworks. With that, I realized that this artist shows that they are dedicated to the things that they do and that idea stuck to my head. When I came to the event there were not a lot of people and I did not see anyone who picked my interest. But I appreciate each artist who puts their time and effort into creating visually appealing artworks. The most difficult part was staying for an hour and there were only limited artworks to look at. But although it was boring, I know it is important to appreciate artwork that was made by local artists since it can help them tremendously. Showing support to different artists will make them feel that there are people who appreciate their artwork and if there are any events similar to this (EXHIBITION) I will show my support. On my end, I don't see any benefit from the event but for the artist, it will show that people are appreciating their artworks since people show up to their exhibition. Also, if there is any possibility that I will do this project I would increase the number of bonsai displayed so that people can explore more.

Because of this activity, I am now aware that even in the Philippines people are doing bonsai artwork and as a Filipino, I think this is important since this type of art requires long amounts of time and it truly shows that Filipinos are capable of doing this works such as this.
